# Heart Health 101: Tips for a Healthier Heart

**Published:** April 21, 2026  
**Author:** Langley Park Pharmacy  
**Reading Time:** 5 minutes

---

Your heart is the most vital organ in your body—it beats roughly 100,000 times a day, pumping blood and oxygen throughout your system. Yet many of us take our heart health for granted until something goes wrong. The good news? There are simple, practical steps you can take right now to keep your heart healthy and strong for years to come.

## Why Heart Health Matters

Heart disease remains one of the leading causes of death worldwide, but here's what many people don't realize: it's often preventable. Your heart's health depends on lifestyle choices you make every single day—what you eat, how much you move, how well you sleep, and how you manage stress.

Taking care of your heart isn't just about avoiding disease. A healthy heart means more energy, better mood, improved sleep, and a higher quality of life overall. It's an investment in your future.

## Common Risk Factors to Watch

Before we talk about solutions, let's identify the risk factors you should be aware of:

- **High blood pressure** – Often called "the silent killer" because you can't feel it
- **High cholesterol** – Builds up in your arteries over time
- **Smoking** – One of the most damaging habits for heart health
- **Excess weight** – Extra pounds put strain on your heart
- **Sedentary lifestyle** – Sitting too much weakens your cardiovascular system
- **Poor diet** – Too much salt, sugar, and unhealthy fats
- **Stress** – Chronic stress raises blood pressure and heart rate
- **Family history** – Genetics play a role, but lifestyle can offset risk
- **Diabetes** – Damages blood vessels over time
- **Excessive alcohol** – Weakens heart muscle

The good news? Most of these factors are within your control.

## Simple Daily Habits for a Healthy Heart

### 1. Move Your Body Regularly

You don't need to run marathons. Aim for **150 minutes of moderate exercise per week**—that's just 30 minutes, 5 days a week. Walking, cycling, swimming, or dancing all count.

Exercise strengthens your heart muscle, improves circulation, helps maintain a healthy weight, and reduces stress. Start where you are and gradually increase activity levels.

### 2. Eat Heart-Healthy Foods

**Focus on:**
- Fresh fruits and vegetables (especially leafy greens, berries, and citrus)
- Whole grains (oats, brown rice, whole wheat bread)
- Lean proteins (fish rich in omega-3s, chicken, legumes)
- Healthy fats (olive oil, avocados, nuts)
- Low-sodium options

**Limit:**
- Processed foods
- Added sugars
- Saturated fats
- Excess salt
- Alcohol

The Mediterranean diet is a great model for heart-healthy eating—it's been shown to reduce heart disease risk significantly.

### 3. Get Quality Sleep

Most adults need **7-9 hours of sleep per night**. During sleep, your body repairs itself and regulates hormones that affect heart health. Poor sleep increases inflammation and raises blood pressure.

Create a sleep routine: go to bed at the same time, avoid screens an hour before sleep, keep your bedroom cool and dark.

### 4. Manage Stress

Chronic stress raises blood pressure and contributes to heart disease. Find what works for you:
- Deep breathing exercises
- Meditation or mindfulness
- Yoga
- Time in nature
- Hobbies you enjoy
- Time with loved ones
- Journaling

Even 10 minutes of daily stress management can make a difference.

### 5. Maintain a Healthy Weight

Excess weight puts extra strain on your heart. If you're overweight, losing just 5-10% of your body weight can improve heart health significantly.

Work with your doctor or a nutritionist on a realistic weight loss plan that includes both diet and exercise.

### 6. Don't Smoke (or Quit if You Do)

Smoking is one of the worst things you can do for your heart. It damages blood vessels, increases blood clots risk, and reduces oxygen in your blood. If you smoke, ask your pharmacist about smoking cessation options—they can help!

### 7. Limit Alcohol

If you drink, do so in moderation. That means up to one drink per day for women, up to two for men. Excessive alcohol weakens the heart muscle and raises blood pressure.

## The Role of Medication & Supplements

Sometimes lifestyle changes alone aren't enough. Your doctor may prescribe medications to manage blood pressure, cholesterol, or other risk factors. These are important—take them as directed.

Certain supplements may help heart health too:
- **Omega-3 fish oil** – Supports healthy cholesterol levels
- **Coenzyme Q10 (CoQ10)** – Supports heart muscle function
- **Magnesium** – Helps regulate heartbeat
- **Garlic** – May help with blood pressure and cholesterol

**Important:** Always talk to your pharmacist before starting any supplement. Some can interact with medications or have side effects.

## When to Seek Professional Help

Contact your doctor if you experience:
- Chest pain or pressure
- Shortness of breath
- Dizziness or fainting
- Unusual fatigue
- Swelling in legs or ankles
- Irregular heartbeat or palpitations

Annual check-ups are also important. Your doctor can check your blood pressure, cholesterol, and overall heart health. If you have risk factors, you may need more frequent monitoring.

## Action Steps You Can Take Today

1. **Schedule a heart check-up** with your doctor (if you haven't had one recently)
2. **Take a 30-minute walk** today
3. **Replace one unhealthy food** with a heart-healthy option
4. **Practice 5 minutes of deep breathing** or meditation
5. **Talk to your pharmacist** about your heart health and any medications or supplements

## The Bottom Line

Heart disease doesn't happen overnight, and heart health doesn't improve overnight either. But it gets better with consistency. Small, daily choices add up to major improvements over weeks and months.

Your heart has been working for you since before you were born. It's time to work for it. Start today—your future self will thank you.
